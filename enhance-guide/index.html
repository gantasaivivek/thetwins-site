Enhancement Guide

What changed in the elevation pass, and why. Nothing was rebuilt — every section, every line of copy, and the world itself carried over untouched. These are surgical enhancements on top of the finished film.

01 · Hero choreography

  • The descriptive sub-copy no longer crosses the figure's legs — it now sits below the open hands, in the clear band above the scroll cue. Why: the site's own rule is "copy around the figure, never across it" — the old top: 68% broke it on tall viewports.
  • Both hero lines gained a soft ice-light halo (text-shadow) and higher resting opacity. Why: 12px mono over sunlit snow was dropping below comfortable contrast.

02 · HUD discipline

  • Past the film, the drifting coordinates, sequence readout and frame corners dim out; they return when you scroll back into the world. Sound/autoplay stay usable at a whisper and come to full voice on hover or focus. Why: fixed telemetry was colliding with the manifesto's live text.
  • On phones the coordinates yield entirely — the sequence readout wins the one line available. Why: both readouts overlapped in 375px viewports, printing garbage like 134.41970W0%.
  • Loader status thresholds fixed — the final line now actually displays (the old integer math capped the index before the last message could show).

03 · The reading deck (specs → manifesto → footer)

  • Cold light now washes down into the specs section and pools at its edges; the manifesto stands under a thin falling light-beam. Why: after the cinematic world, the page fell into a flat grey void — the atmosphere must never die.
  • Spec cells wake under the hand: the hairline glass brightens and the number lifts with a cold glow. Counters race up with more drama and seat themselves with a breath of light on arrival.
  • The manifesto's one warm phrase — a human meant it — draws its amber underline the moment the page is read.
  • The footer's idle particles now orbit as a quiet banded halo instead of a figure-eight scribble. Hovering any footer link still condenses them into that link's relic (heart, key, dart, hourglass, beacon, lattice, twins).

04 · Glow UI — the pulse made literal

  • The 3D world publishes the human heartbeat (the same lub-dub driving the red gem) to the page as a CSS variable, --pulse, every frame.
  • The HUD frame corners and the wordmark's star breathe with it — the whole instrument shares one heartbeat with the world. Why: this is the Glow UI concept executed with zero new assets — pulse-reactive interface, driven by the story's own heart.

05 · Micro-interactions

  • Magnetic pull on nav links, social icons, footer links and HUD toggles — they lean a few pixels toward the cursor and spring home elastically.
  • A fine survey ring drifts after the cursor and opens over anything interactive. The native cursor is never hidden. Touch devices and reduced-motion users never see either behaviour.

06 · Brand voice

  • The finalized tagline — YOUR TWIN. YOUR RULES. — now closes the loader sequence (the last line read before the world opens) and anchors the footer meta column. It appeared nowhere on the page before.
  • Palette, wordmark, logo treatment, copy, and the arctic monochrome discipline (one red heart, one cyan signal, one amber warning) are untouched.

07 · What was deliberately NOT done

  • No WebGL text, no new fonts, no dark-void reskin, no generated video loops. Why: the site's power is its real-time world and its DOM accessibility (a real heading tree, skip links, focus rings — the axis where igloo.inc conceded points). Generated video would fight the live renderer, and the arctic-light identity is the brand, not a costume to swap.
